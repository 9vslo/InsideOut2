👥 About InsideOut

Who We Are
We’re a student-led awareness initiative created from a real technical report conducted at Khalifa University in Spring 2025. InsideOut was born from research, but driven by emotion. It’s a space where KU students can explore the hidden pressures of university life—comparison, confidence, belonging, and mental well-being.

Our Mission
To build a campus culture rooted in emotional honesty—not just achievement. We believe that being real is more powerful than being perfect.

Our Goals:

  • Normalize open conversations about pressure and self-doubt
  • Help students see they’re not alone in feeling behind
  • Use storytelling, data, and design to support student wellness
  • Shift KU culture from competition to connection

📚 The Pressure Beneath the Surface

Background (Based on Literature Review)
Khalifa University is known for excellence. But behind that excellence lies a quiet struggle: students comparing themselves to peers in academics, leadership, social life, and even personality. According to social comparison theory, this is natural—but in highly competitive spaces, it becomes harmful.

Your research shows:

  • Students often feel like their achievements don’t matter when surrounded by "high performers."
  • Upward comparison can decrease confidence, increase anxiety, and even cause imposter syndrome.
  • Students who constantly compare themselves feel less connected, avoid social engagement, and hesitate to ask for help.

The Illusion of Perfection
When everyone looks put-together, students assume they’re the only ones struggling. But they’re not. That illusion is isolating—and dangerous.

📊 What Our Research Revealed

Findings From Our KU Student Survey (N = 24)

  • ✔️ Social Comparison is Frequent: Most students rated their comparison frequency between 6–9 out of 10.
  • ✔️ Confidence Suffers: 55% said comparison lowered their confidence. Only 5% said it increased it.
  • ✔️ Belonging is Affected: As comparison increased, students were less likely to feel they belonged.
  • ✔️ Imposter Syndrome is Real: 37.5% experience it sometimes, 33% often or always.
  • ✔️ The Illusion Has Power: Students who believe others "have it figured out" are more hesitant to ask for help, engage, or join groups.

Conclusion?
What students believe about their peers shapes what they do. This illusion causes withdrawal, doubt, and loneliness.

💡 Our Recommendations

How can KU reduce this pressure and improve well-being?

  • 🎙️ Start a student-led podcast — Normalize “figuring it out” as a process.
  • 🌐 Create a digital confession wall — Real, anonymous stories from KU students.
  • 🧏‍♀️ Train faculty to recognize burnout signs — Just one kind check-in can shift a student’s entire week.
  • ☕ Host monthly “circle talks” — Informal, safe, honest, and non-judgmental spaces.
  • 🎨 Use storytelling in KU events — Highlight growth journeys, not just polished outcomes.
